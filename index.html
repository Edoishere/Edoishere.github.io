Projects

Integrating XAI for Predictive Conflict Analytics

Integrating XAI for Predictive Conflict Analytics explores the development of an AI-powered Early Warning System (AI-EWS) for forecasting global conflict fatalities. Using transformer-based models and the ACLED dataset, it predicts weekly fatalities across 168 countries. To enhance transparency, the study integrates Integrated Gradients, an XAI technique that makes predictions interpretable for policymakers. Presented at xAI-2024, this work demonstrates how explainable AI can support responsible, data-driven conflict prevention.

Master Thesis: Causal Machine Learning for Survival Analysis

Developed advanced methods for estimating survival curves and quantile treatment effects under right-censored data. The project integrates causal inference with machine learning to enhance predictive performance and interpretability in survival analysis tasks. A key contribution is the extension of large-sample properties—consistency and asymptotic normality—from the survival estimator to the quantile estimator. This is achieved via the functional delta method, allowing for valid inference and the construction of proper confidence intervals around quantile treatment effect estimates.
